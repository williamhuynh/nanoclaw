# Complementary Design / AI Delivery Partnership

## Pattern

Two firms with adjacent but non-overlapping capability sets form an ongoing joint-pitch alliance, not a one-off collaboration:

- **Partner A** — CX/UX, design, mobile / product build. Not AI-native.
- **Partner B** — AI strategy, governance, delivery. Not design / build-native.

Neither firm extends outside its core; together they can win engagements that require *both* an experience play and an AI play, which neither could credibly deliver alone.

## Why it works

- **Service boundary is sharp** — no capability overlap, so no internal competition on scope
- **Clients frequently need both** — modern digital engagements rarely stop at "design only" or "AI only"
- **Lower go-to-market cost** — each partner qualifies opportunities from its own pipeline, then pulls the other in when the shape fits
- **Credibility transfer** — each firm inherits trust from the other's existing relationship

## Structural conditions for the pattern to work

- Complementary, *not adjacent-competing*, service portfolios (design vs delivery vs AI strategy — not e.g. "two consultancies who both do data")
- Personal trust between founders / senior partners — the pattern is relationship-led, not contractually enforced
- At least one live joint opportunity in the pipeline at any time — keeps the partnership active rather than theoretical
- Both firms accept that the other may have other design / AI partners too (usually non-exclusive)

## Open design questions (formalising the pattern)

When the partnership commercialises beyond pitch-level, the following need to be decided:

- **Who leads?** — usually whichever partner originated the client
- **Margin split on joint work** — resourcing-based, flat share, or rev-share model
- **Naming / positioning to clients** — joint brand, one-lead-one-sub, or "allied partners"
- **Exclusivity** — is either firm precluded from using other partners on the other side?
- **Cross-sell pathways** — does the relationship extend across the parent groups of either firm?

## Pipeline-screening implication

When qualifying a new opportunity, ask early: *does this client need an experience / design dimension alongside the AI work?* If yes, default to considering a joint pitch before either an in-house extension or a non-partnered alternative.

## Confidence

High on the existence and longevity of the partnership archetype — it recurs across AiD's pipeline. Medium on the *commercial formalisation* shape — most partnerships of this kind stay semi-informal until a breakthrough engagement forces the commercial question.
