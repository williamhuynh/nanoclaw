# Westpac

## Overview
- **Type:** Client (prospective — proposal stage)
- **Engagement:** Optifusion proposal — research into customer attitudes toward AI in banking
- **Status:** Proposal planning (sessions 13–14 Apr 2026)
- **Relationship to AiD:** AiD providing AI team expertise within a broader Optifusion-led proposal

## Goal of the Work
- Foundational, unbiased insight into how Australian customers feel about AI in banking — especially handing off financial execution to AI agents
- Current AI decisions at Westpac are made on personal opinion; they want real customer evidence to guide design and policy
- Research will be a key input into a broader "human simulator" that predicts customer reactions to products and experiences

## Human Simulator Vision
- Westpac already has "many agents" doing siloed tasks; they want to orchestrate them into a holistic simulator of customer behaviour
- Use cases:
  - Input a concept (e.g. new loan feature) → get probability of customer need, pain points, segment impacts (vulnerable, First Nations, small business, etc.)
  - Identify research gaps and avoid duplicative research
  - Help designers in real time during delivery conversations to challenge/validate ideas

## AI, Trust, and Boundaries
- Internal principle: AI should offer options, not make decisions; humans remain accountable
- Westpac wants to understand:
  - Where customers are comfortable with AI vs. requiring humans
  - How transparent they must be ("AI-enabled service" labels, explicit choice vs. defaulting to AI)
  - The "edge" where innovation is acceptable without breaching ethical or comfort boundaries
- Example tension: using AI to read payslips today and simply tagging "this is an AI-enabled service" — they don't know if that reassures or scares customers

## Defining "AI" for Customers
- Concern that customers equate "AI" mainly with ChatGPT-like tools; many AI-driven features (spellcheck, recommendations) aren't seen as AI
- Risk: research asking "how do you feel about AI?" may only measure media-driven associations, not real behaviour
- Need to clarify what "AI" means for Westpac and explore how customers define/experience AI in practical use cases

## Research Methodology Expectations
- Qualitative: deep contextual work (multi-week "behavioral missions", ethnographic techniques) to observe real behaviour around AI in financial and adjacent tasks
- Quantitative: scalable survey to validate patterns across segments
