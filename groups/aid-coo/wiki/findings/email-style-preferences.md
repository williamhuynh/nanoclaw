# Email Style Preferences — Will

Style preferences for any email drafts for Will (AiD business emails, external stakeholder communication).

## Rules

- **No em-dashes (—).** Use hyphens (-) or restructure the sentence.
- **Length:** Short. Long emails "look not leadership." Target ~100-150 words for substantive replies; shorter for acknowledgements.
- **No over-explaining.** Say the thing once and stop. Trust the reader to infer obvious context.
- **No next-step asks when the ask is obvious.** If the ball is clearly in their court, don't spell it out.
- **Strength, not ego.** Avoid "conditional on", "need", "must", gatekeeper phrasing.
- **Collaborative framing over self-protective.** Frame asks as being in the joint/business interest, not Will's interest. E.g., "for X to be successful" beats "my support is conditional on...".
- **Calibrate tone to audience register.** When writing to people who are effectively part of the team (e.g., Edge Red / Charlotte, Bound Interactive), drop emotive / selling language. No "I think you'll recognise", "resonates strongly", "to good effect", "would be good to". Write like you're messaging a colleague: matter-of-fact, slightly informal, no pitching. Save the polished/external register for clients and new prospects.
- **Lead with commercial context when the recipient needs it to act.** For internal-tier partners (Edge Red, Bound), don't assume they can decode the ask from the tactical request alone. Open with the pipeline shape ("separate AI tranche ongoing, one additional piece we're trying to open up as a separate opportunity"), name the stakeholder and their live question, state commitments already made to the client ("I said I'll share a few overview slides"), and surface the commercial intent explicitly ("trusted advisor... perfect opportunity to shape some work with her"). Trade brevity for context when context is what lets them decide whether to invest effort. Charlotte email (23 Apr) ran ~230 words for this reason — brevity rule yields to the "enough shape to act on" rule.
- **Tenet chain for decisions-first data framing** (verbatim from Will's sent email): *decisions → frequency, consequences and materiality of those decisions → insight needed → data required to power that.* Use this chain intact in any future data-for-AI positioning; don't abbreviate it.
- **Collaborative-first ask, existing-collateral fallback.** "Hoping to work with you and team to put something together, or if you already have one-pagers that we could tailor and share?" (collab first). Previous draft inverted this (existing first, crit as fallback) — Will's version reads as warmer and more partnership-y.

## Preferred phrases (observed)

- "Supportive in principle"
- "Keen to see..."
- "Would be good to see..."
- "Consistent with our agreed approach to..."
- "Happy to move quickly with a structured approach"
- "hasn't been discussed yet" (beats "wasn't agreed")

## Phrases to avoid

- "As per our discussion on..." (reads as finger-pointing — rephrase as "Consistent with our agreed approach to...")
- "My support is conditional on..."
- "Need a plan / resource plan / R&R before locking anything in"
- Long justifications of team workload
- Emotive / pitching language when writing to internal-tier recipients: "resonates strongly", "to good effect", "I think you'll recognise", "would be good to share something that makes the case cleanly"

## Structure template for process-reset replies (tested on LIRS reply)

```
Thanks for the note. Supportive in principle and keen to work through how we land this well [positive framing of the opportunity].

A few things to work through first:

1. [Factual correction — "hasn't been discussed yet"]
2. [Scope/plan ask — anchored in agreed principle]
3. [Fair sharing / team framing — "for X to be successful, it can't sit with..."]
[Optional 4. Pull info/work that already exists before asking for more]

Happy to move quickly with a structured approach.

Cheers,
Will
```
