# Westpac Human Simulator — Strategic Alignment with AiD

## Finding
Westpac's "human simulator" vision — orchestrating multiple AI agents into a holistic customer behaviour predictor — maps almost exactly to AiD's proposed persona simulation prototype. This is a strong strategic alignment.

## Evidence
- Westpac wants to input a concept and get back predicted customer reactions across segments
- They want to identify research gaps and help designers validate ideas in real time
- AiD's pitch: build personas from research data to test more design questions — same capability, different framing

## Implications
- AiD's prototype pitch isn't just a nice-to-have add-on; it's a demonstration of the client's end-state vision
- If the prototype lands well, it could open a follow-on engagement for building the full human simulator
- The SOPs agent / knowledge corpus work is a prerequisite — the simulator needs structured, AI-consumable research data as input
- This validates AiD's positioning: output design for AI consumption is not peripheral, it's foundational to Westpac's strategy

## Confidence
High — based on direct client statements in the Q&A session
