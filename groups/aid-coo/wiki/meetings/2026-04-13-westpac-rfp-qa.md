# Westpac RFP Q&A — AI Trust & Human Simulator

**Date:** ~13 Apr 2026 (notes shared 13 Apr)
**Context:** Q&A session for the Westpac Optifusion RFP
**Key people:** Kwafo (leading), Oz, Carla, Will (partial), Cynthia (briefed)

---

## Goal of the Work
- Westpac wants foundational, unbiased insight into how Australian customers feel about AI in banking — especially when handing off financial execution to AI agents
- Current AI-related decisions are being made on personal opinion; they want real customer evidence to guide design and policy
- This research will be a key input into a broader "human simulator" that predicts customer reactions

## Human Simulator Vision
- Westpac already has "many agents" doing siloed tasks; want to orchestrate them into a holistic customer behaviour simulator
- Use cases:
  - Input a concept (e.g. new loan feature) → get probability of customer need, pain points, segment impacts (vulnerable, First Nations, small business, etc.)
  - Identify research gaps, avoid duplicative research
  - Help designers in real time during delivery conversations to challenge/validate ideas

## AI, Trust, and Boundaries
- Internal principle: AI should offer options, not make decisions; humans remain accountable
- Key questions:
  - Where are customers comfortable with AI vs. requiring humans?
  - How transparent must they be? ("AI-enabled service" labels, explicit choice vs. defaulting to AI)
  - Where is the "edge" where innovation is acceptable without breaching ethical/comfort boundaries?
- Tension example: using AI to read payslips today — tagging "this is an AI-enabled service" — unclear if that reassures or scares customers

## Defining "AI" for Customers vs. Bank
- Concern: customers may equate "AI" mainly with ChatGPT-like tools; many AI-driven features (spellcheck, recommendations) aren't perceived as AI
- Risk: asking "how do you feel about AI?" may only measure media-driven associations, not real behaviour
- Need to clarify what "AI" means for Westpac and how customers define/experience AI in practical use cases

## Research Methodology
- Qualitative: deep contextual work — multi-week "behavioral missions", ethnographic techniques to observe real behaviour around AI in financial and adjacent tasks
- Quantitative: scalable survey to validate patterns across segments
